### Why Document This?

When I analysed different sourcesâ€”forum posts, judicial decisions, professional guidance, FOI data, and gaps in recordsâ€”these six types kept emerging. This isn't a claim of universal truth or complete categorization. It's **triangulation made explicit**: naming which angle you're looking from makes research more traceable and testable.

**Key principles:**
- No lens is "more true" than another (a parent's story = as valid as statistical data)
- More lenses = more robust understanding
- Missing lenses = blind spots
- Categories themselves remain open to challenge

### How to Use

1. When collecting evidence, ask each lens's question
2. Tag which lens(es) each piece of evidence comes through
3. Note when something fits none of them (signals new thinking needed)
4. Document which lenses are absent from your analysis

### Testing

After practical use, this framework will either:
- Prove useful and be refined
- Reveal missing/unnecessary categories
- Be replaced by something better
